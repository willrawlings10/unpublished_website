I am interested in product design, software engineering, and helping others through design and technology. Feel free to explore some of my past and current projects!

Camera trap with embedded Edge AI

In conjunction with Mia de Leon, I created a low-powered camera trap, or camera triggered by motion, with an embedded machine learning algorithm optimized for mobile deployment. The algorithm could identify White-Tailed deer with 87.5% accuracy and trigger an alert to a base station in response to a positive identification. The project was started as part of an internship at Conservation X Labs in June 2019 and was continued in the fall as an independent research project supervised by Dr. Nan Jokerst at Duke University. The project concluded in December 2019 with a proof of concept device.

Audacity Labs: Lead Program Developer (Computer Science and Engineering)

Audacity Labs is a non-profit focused on enhancing the education of 9-12th graders through outreach programs focusing on innovation, entrepreneurship, and the introduction of technical skills that are not traditionally taught in high school classrooms. As a program developer for STEM, I create the curriculum for introductory computer science and engineering modules, aimed for 2020 implementation. Modules that are currently in development include: Computer-Aided design 101, Intro to Arduinos, and Programming in Python.

EGR 121 Final Project: Mario themed Rube-Goldberg machine   

For the final design project of EGR 121: Desiging Innovation, our team of 13 built a  Mario themed Rube-Goldberg machine that consisted of 32 ft of track. The group was split into 4 sub-groups, with each given a specific "world" in Mario. As part of the "Mushroom World" subteam, I builted and soldered a circuoit in which an ultrasonic sensor would trigger a motor to turn a scissor lift, which was built by my partner. In addition, I built and coded a circuit that powered an LED screen to display "Insert coin here." The final project was a testament to communication, collaboration, project management, and systems integration.

EGR 101 Design Project: A movement aid solution for geriatric lemurs  

In EGR 101, students choose a client with a design problem and spend the semester iterating towards a solution. In collaboration with the Duke Lemur Center, my group was to create a movement aid system for geriatric lemurs, as their age limited their scope of movement. In response, our team built a foldable, height-adjustable ramp using only PVC pipe, vinyl-coated wire mesh, and zipties. The The project was a lesson in low-resource design, as zinc is poisonous to lemurs and greatly limited the available materials. The solution, along with instructions for assembly and reproduction,  was presented to the Duke Lemur Center in December 2018 for integration into the center.
