Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory

During the Summer of 2020, I worked as a (remote) Summer Engineering Analyst at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory. I was brought on to help build a finite element preprocessor under Dr. Patrick Lea in the Advanced Engineering Analysis Group. I was primarily using Object-Oriented Programming in Python to develop a unit tracking and conversion system for LLNL's future modelling software. During my time there, I implemented and tracked 47 units, 8 unit systems, and 139 unit conversions for Length, Mass, Time, and Temperature and developed a comprehensive testing suite (250+ tests) using Pytest. I presented my work to the Advanced Engineering Analysis Group, resulting in approval and merging of the code into the product in August 2020.

Conservation X Labs

In conjunction with Mia de Leon, I created a low-powered camera trap, or camera triggered by motion, with an embedded machine learning algorithm optimized for mobile deployment. The algorithm could identify White-Tailed deer with 94% accuracy and trigger an alert to a base station in response to a positive identification. The project was started as part of an internship at Conservation X Labs in June 2019 and was continued in the fall as an independent research project supervised by Dr. Nan Jokerst at Duke University. The project concluded in December 2019 with a proof of concept device.

EGR 101 Design Project: A movement aid solution for geriatric lemurs  

In EGR 101, students choose a client with a design problem and spend the semester iterating towards a solution. In collaboration with the Duke Lemur Center, my group was tasked with designing a movement aid system for geriatric lemurs, as their age limited their scope of movement. In response, our team built a foldable, height-adjustable ramp using only PVC pipe, vinyl-coated wire mesh, and zipties. The project was a lesson in low-resource design, as zinc is poisonous to lemurs and greatly limited the available materials. The solution, along with instructions for assembly and reproduction,  was presented to the Duke Lemur Center in December 2018 for integration into the center.
